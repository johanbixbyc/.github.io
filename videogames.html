About

Story of Oth is an Open-World RPG. The design philosophy of the game centers around bringing writing, game mechanics, and lore, together in ways that make every element of the game feel necessary and deep, and giving the player a streamlined, logical, and most importantly, unified experience.

Some plants in the game present the player with opportunities to harvest them. The game features a variety of harvestables and collectibles, such as plants, ores, fossils, artifacts, and more. Each and every harvestable and collectible has a lore background and has functionable properties, either as a stem for dialogue with specific NPCs (an archaelogist for example may be interested in ancient carvings), craftable, or otherwise useful.


The player enters dialogue with a quest giving NPC. In this quest, the NPC had been brought an ancient tablet carved with an unknown variant of a long-dead language. The NPC lies to the man who had brought him the tablet, as the man seemed untrustworthy, and said he couldn't make out any of the text. However, the NPC tells the player he could make out a little: directions to a cave West of a penninsula such as the one they are currently standing on. The NPC implores the player to go to the cave and bring back whatever they can find. Throughout the rest of the quest, the player learns that the cave was an important location for a cult of ancient necromancers trying to resurrect a nearly forgotten, and exceptionally evil, man who had long ago apotheosized into a god that the necromancers worshipped. The man who had brought the tablet to the NPC is a member of the remnants of that cult, and tries to revive the ancient evil. The player may choose to either join the necromancers' cause or defeat the necromancers.
